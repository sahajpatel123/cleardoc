Original · §4.2 Indemnification ClearDoc
The party of the first part shall indemnify and hold harmless the party of the second part from and against any and all liabilities, claims, damages, and expenses, including reasonable attorneys' fees, arising out of or in connection with any breach notwithstanding any provision herein to the contrary, and such obligation shall survive the termination in perpetuity.
What this means for you:

If you break this agreement, you have to pay for any losses the other side suffers — including their legal fees.

This stays true even after the contract ends, with no time limit.

⚠ watch this "in perpetuity" means forever.
